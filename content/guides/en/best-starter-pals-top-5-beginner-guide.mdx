# Palworld Best Starter Pals: Top 5 Beginner Guide

Starting your journey in Palworld can feel overwhelming with over 100 Pals to discover. Picking the right starter Pal can make or break your first few hours of gameplay, determining whether you struggle through early bosses or breeze through them. This Palworld best starter Pals top 5 beginner guide breaks down exactly which Pals you should prioritize catching first, where to find them, and why each one earns its spot on this list.

Whether you are a complete newcomer or returning after a break, these five Pals will carry you through the early game and remain useful well into the mid-game. Each one was chosen based on availability, utility, combat power, and how well they complement a beginner's playstyle.

## Why Your Starter Pal Matters in Palworld

Your first Pal is more than just a companion — it shapes your entire early-game experience. The Pal you choose affects:

- **How quickly you progress** through the starting zones
- **Which bosses you can tackle** first
- **Your resource gathering speed** in the critical first hour
- **Your confidence** learning the game's combat and breeding systems

> **Pro Tip:** Don't waste your first few hours chasing rare Pals. A solid starter Pal handled well will outperform a rare Pal at low levels because you can level it up and learn its moveset immediately.

Unlike other creature-collection games, Palworld rewards you for sticking with your starter and investing in it. The bonding system means a well-cared-for common Pal can outclass an neglected legendary one in the early hours.

## Top 5 Best Starter Pals in Palworld

Here is our ranked list of the five best Pals every beginner should aim to catch in the first 30 minutes of gameplay.

### 1. Rushoar — The Tanky Boar

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Earth |
| Partner Skill | Hard Head — Increases attack when riding |
| Best Use | Combat tank, mining assistance |
| Catch Location | Starting plateau, windswept hills |
| Recommended Level | 2-5 |

Rushoar is the undisputed king of starter Pals. This boar-type Earth Pal is available almost immediately after you spawn, and its partner skill Hard Head gives you a damage boost while riding it. What makes Rushoar exceptional is its durability — it can take hits from early bosses that would knock out other low-level Pals.

Rushoar also has a hidden talent for mining. Its Earth-type partner skill speeds up ore gathering, meaning you spend less time grinding for basic resources. Catch at least two Rushoars early on: one for combat and one for base work.

### 2. Cattiva — The Versatile Cat

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Neutral |
| Partner Skill | Cat Helper — Boosts work speed at base |
| Best Use | Base worker, generalist |
| Catch Location | Everywhere in starting area |
| Recommended Level | 1-10 |

Cattiva might look unassuming, but this Neutral-type cat Pal is the Swiss Army knife of early Palworld. Its partner skill, Cat Helper, increases work speed at your base, making it invaluable once you establish your first settlement.

Cattiva can perform nearly every task at your base, from crafting to farming. While it is not the strongest in combat, having two or three Cattivas in your base means your resource production stays steady while you are out exploring.

> **Pro Tip:** Cattivas are cheap to feed and easy to breed. Keep a few in your base even in the mid-game as reliable support workers.

### 3. Pengullet — The Fire-Breathing Penguin

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Ice / Fire (dual affinity) |
| Partner Skill | Waddle Dash — Faster travel while riding |
| Best Use | Fast mount, fire combat |
| Catch Location | Frozen shores, northern beaches |
| Recommended Level | 3-7 |

Pengullet brings something no other starter Pal can match: speed. Its Waddle Dash partner skill makes it the fastest early-game mount, letting you traverse the map much faster than running on foot.

Despite being an Ice-type visually, Pengullet has access to fire attacks, giving you type coverage against Grass and Ice enemies. This dual nature makes it surprisingly versatile in early boss fights.

### 4. Teafant — The Water Tank

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Water |
| Partner Skill | Soaking Shell — Water attack boost when riding |
| Best Use | Water combat, base hydration |
| Catch Location | Coastal areas, river banks |
| Recommended Level | 2-6 |

Teafant is the Water-type starter you want on your team from day one. While it may not be the flashiest Pal, its Soaking Shell partner skill gives a solid damage boost in water-heavy areas, and Water is one of the most useful types in the early game.

Teafant excels at putting out fires at your base and is effective against the many Fire-type enemies you encounter in the first few zones. Its evolution line leads to Penking, one of the best mid-game Pals, making early investment worthwhile.

### 5. Sparkit — The Electric Utility Pal

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Electric |
| Partner Skill | Spark Maker — Unlocks early electricity crafting |
| Best Use | Base power generation, Electric combat |
| Catch Location | Grassland areas, near power sources |
| Recommended Level | 2-5 |

Sparkit unlocks one of the most important base features: electricity. Its partner skill lets you access Electric-type crafting recipes earlier than other Pals would allow. Having a Sparkit at your base means you can start building electrical components and advanced equipment faster.

Electric is also a strong offensive type in Palworld, effective against Water and Flying enemies. Sparkit's attacks are fast and reliable, making it a solid combat companion during your first few boss fights.

## How to Build Your Early Game Team

Once you have your starter Pal, here is how to build a balanced team for the first 10 hours of gameplay.

### Step 1: Catch Your Main Starter (Minutes 1-5)

Head straight for the nearest Rushoar spawn point. This should be your primary combat companion for the first few hours. Level it up by defeating wild Pals and completing early quests.

### Step 2: Add a Base Worker (Minutes 5-15)

Catch two or three Cattivas for your base. They will handle crafting, mining, and farming while you are away exploring. Do not skip this step — a well-staffed base accelerates your progress dramatically.

### Step 3: Fill Type Gaps (Minutes 15-30)

Depending on your playstyle, add either a Teafant for Water coverage or a Sparkit for Electric coverage. Having at least two different types on your team means you can handle more enemy varieties.

### Step 4: Upgrade and Evolve (Hours 2-10)

Focus on leveling your main combat Pal first. Once it reaches level 15-20, start spreading experience to your secondary Pals. Begin thinking about breeding combinations that can improve your team's passive skills.

## Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

Even with the right starter Pal, beginners often make these costly mistakes:

**Over-relying on one Pal.** Your starter will carry you early, but you need a team of at least three Pals for boss fights. Rotate them to share experience.

**Ignoring base workers.** A strong explorer means nothing if your base is empty. Catch those Cattivas immediately.

**Not feeding your Pals.** Hungry Pals perform worse and can become unhappy. Keep berries stocked at all times.

**Skipping the breeding system.** Breeding lets you create Pals with better passive skills. Start experimenting after your first few hours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the absolute best starter Pal in Palworld?

Rushoar is widely considered the best starter Pal due to its high durability, early availability, mining assistance, and strong partner skill. It can handle most early-game challenges and remains useful longer than other starters.

### Can I change my starter Pal later?

Yes, you can catch any Pal at any time. Your "starter" is simply the first Pal you catch. We recommend catching multiple Pals from our top 5 list within your first 30 minutes for the best experience.

### Do starter Pals evolve?

Some starter Pals evolve into stronger forms. Teafant evolves into Penking, which is one of the strongest mid-game Pals. Rushoar and Cattiva do not evolve but can be bred for improved offspring.

### What if I already started the game with a different Pal?

You can still follow this guide. Simply head to the locations listed above and catch the recommended Pals. You can release or reassign your original starter to base duty while you build a stronger team.

### Are these starter Pals still useful in the late game?

Cattiva remains useful as a base worker throughout the entire game. Rushoar's evolution line is competitive into the mid-game. Pengullet and Sparkit can be replaced by stronger Pals later, but they serve you well for the first 15-20 hours of gameplay.

### How do I make my starter Pal stronger faster?

Focus on feeding it, using it in combat regularly, and equipping it with the best gear you can craft. Bonus experience is gained when your Pal defeats enemies of a higher level, so challenging slightly stronger foes is an effective leveling strategy.
