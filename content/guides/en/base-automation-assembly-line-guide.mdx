# Palworld Base Automation Assembly Line — Work Pals & Production Chain Guide

Building a fully automated base in Palworld is one of the most satisfying endgame achievements you can accomplish. Imagine logging into your world and watching your Pals tirelessly craft ammunition, smelt ingots, bake bread, and assemble complex components — all without you lifting a finger. A well-designed assembly line base transforms your operation from a chaotic jumble of workers into a precision production machine that outputs thousands of items per in-game day.

This comprehensive guide walks you through everything you need to know about base automation in Palworld: optimal base layout design, how to set up efficient production chains, which Pals make the best workers for each job, and the expert-level automation tips that separate casual bases from true endgame factories. Whether you're just starting to plan your megabase or looking to optimize an existing setup, this guide has you covered.

## Understanding Base Automation Fundamentals

Before diving into layouts and production chains, it's essential to understand how Palworld's automation system works at a mechanical level.

### How Pal Workers Operate

Each Pal in your base has a set of **work suitability ratings** that determine how efficiently they perform specific tasks. These ratings range from 0 to 3 (with some exceptional Pals reaching higher), and they directly affect crafting speed and output quality. The key work categories are:

| Work Type | Description | Key Stats |
|---|---|---|
| Crafting | General item crafting at workbenches | Crafting Speed, Dexterity |
| Smelting | Ore refinement at smelters and furnaces | Heat Resistance, Strength |
| Cooking | Food preparation at cooking stations | Fire/Water affinity |
| Logging | Wood gathering from trees | Strength, Size |
| Mining | Ore and stone gathering | Strength, digging ability |
| Farming | Crop planting and harvesting | Water affinity, patience |
| Medicine | Potion and medical item creation | Intelligence, Dexterity |

### The SAN (Sanity) System

Pals lose SAN as they work. When SAN drops too low, their efficiency plummets and they may refuse to work entirely. An effective automation system must account for SAN management through:

- **Adequate food supply** — Different foods restore different amounts of SAN
- **Rest areas** — Beds and Pal boxes allow Pals to recover
- **Hot springs** — Some bases include hot springs for rapid SAN recovery
- **Work-rest scheduling** — Rotating Pals between active work and rest periods

> **Pro Tip:** Always keep multiple Pals assigned to each production station. When one Pal's SAN drops, a fresh replacement can step in while the tired one recovers. This keeps your assembly line running around the clock without interruption.

## Base Layout Design for Maximum Efficiency

The physical layout of your base has a massive impact on automation efficiency. Pals need to move between storage, workstations, and rest areas — minimizing travel time is the single biggest optimization you can make.

### The Hub-and-Spoke Design

The most efficient base layout in Palworld follows a hub-and-spoke pattern:

```
                    [Rest Quarters]
                         |
[Raw Storage] --- [Central Hub] --- [Smelting Bay]
                         |
                  [Crafting Floor]
                         |
                  [Finished Storage]
```

**Central Hub:** The core of your base where Pals transition between tasks. Place your primary storage chests here for easy access.

**Spokes:** Each spoke is a dedicated production zone — smelting, crafting, cooking, medicine, etc. Pals travel from the hub to their assigned spoke, work, then return to the hub (or rest quarters) when done.

### Zoning for Production Chains

A production chain is a sequence of crafting steps where the output of one workstation becomes the input for the next. For example:

**Ammunition Production Chain:**
1. Mining station produces ore
2. Smelting station converts ore to ingots
3. Crafting station combines ingots into components
4. Assembly station creates finished ammunition

Place each step of the chain adjacent to the next so Pals spend minimal time transporting materials between stations.

### Recommended Base Dimensions

| Base Scale | Footprint | Capacity | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter Base | 30x30 | 10-15 Pals | Early game survival |
| Medium Base | 60x60 | 25-40 Pals | Mid-game production |
| Megabase | 100x100+ | 60-120 Pals | Full endgame automation |

For serious automation, plan for a megabase footprint of at least 100x100. This gives you room to expand production lines as you unlock new recipes and technologies.

## Production Chains — What to Automate First

Not all items are worth automating immediately. Focus on production chains that provide the highest value per unit of time invested. Here's a priority ranking:

### Priority 1: Ammunition and Weapons

Ammunition is consumed constantly and never stops being needed. A dedicated ammo production chain ensures you never run dry during combat or boss fights.

**Chain:** Ore → Ingot → Gun Components → Ammunition

**Recommended workstation count:**
- 3-4 Smelters for ingot production
- 4-5 Crafting benches for component assembly
- 2-3 dedicated ammo assembly stations

### Priority 2: Building Materials and Pal Spheres

Building materials are needed continuously for base expansion and repairs. Pal Spheres are essential for catching new Pals.

**Chain for refined wood:** Logs → Wood Blocks → Refined Components
**Chain for Pal Spheres:** Ore → Ingot → Pal Sphere Components → Assembly

### Priority 3: Food and Medicine

Automated food production keeps your entire Pal workforce fed without manual intervention. Medicine production ensures you always have healing supplies.

**Chain for food:** Crops → Berries → Cooked Meals
**Chain for medicine:** Herbs + Mushrooms → Low-Grade Medicine → High-Grade Medicine

### Priority 4: Advanced and Late-Game Materials

Once your basic chains are running smoothly, expand into advanced production:

- Technology points farming items
- Legendary crafting materials
- Trading goods for merchant exchange

> **Pro Tip:** Start your automation with ammunition and basic building materials. These two chains alone will eliminate 80% of the manual crafting tedium and free you up to explore and build more.

## Expert Automation Tips for Endgame Bases

Once you've got the basics down, these advanced strategies will push your base to peak efficiency:

### 1. Implement a Pal Rotation System

Don't let the same Pals work indefinitely. Create a rotation schedule where Pals alternate between active duty and rest. A simple approach:

- **Group A:** Works 12 in-game hours, then rests 12 hours
- **Group B:** Works 12 in-game hours (opposite schedule from Group A)

This ensures every workstation always has a fully-rested Pal operating at peak efficiency.

### 2. Optimize Food Supply Chains

Your workers need food to maintain SAN. Set up an automated food pipeline:

1. Farming Pals grow crops continuously
2. Cooking Pals convert crops into high-SAN-restoration meals
3. Storage Pals distribute food to feeding stations near work areas

**Best foods for worker SAN recovery:**
- Mushroom stew (easy to produce in bulk)
- Grilled meat pies (high SAN restoration per unit)
- Salads (fast to prepare, decent SAN value)

### 3. Use Conveyor Systems Wisely

While Palworld doesn't have literal conveyor belts, you can simulate assembly line flow by strategically placing storage chests between workstations:

- **Input chest** at each workstation — Pals automatically pull materials from here
- **Output chest** at each workstation — Finished products are deposited here
- **Buffer chests** between stages — Prevent bottlenecks when one stage is faster than another

### 4. Monitor and Adjust Production Ratios

If your ammunition assembly station is always waiting for components but your smelters are backed up with excess ingots, you have a production ratio problem. Adjust the number of Pals assigned to each stage until material flow is balanced.

**Rule of thumb:** The slowest step in your chain is your bottleneck. Add more Pals to that step first.

### 5. Separate Combat Pals from Workers

Don't assign your best combat Pals to base work unless they also have high work suitability. Keep combat specialists in your active party and fill base roles with dedicated workers. This ensures your fighting team stays battle-ready while your workers stay production-ready.

### 6. Build Multiple Specialized Bases

Instead of one megabase that does everything, consider building specialized bases:

| Base | Primary Function | Key Workers |
|---|---|---|
| Main Megabase | General production, ammo, materials | Crafters and smelters |
| Farm Base | Food, herbs, crop production | Farming Pals |
| Mining Outpost | Ore and stone extraction | Mining Pals |
| Research Base | Technology and medicine | Intelligence Pals |

Specialized bases allow you to concentrate the best workers for each task and reduce travel overhead.

## Common Automation Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced players make these errors when building automated bases:

1. **Not planning for expansion.** Build your base with room to grow. Cramming new production lines into an already-full base creates inefficiency.

2. **Ignoring SAN management.** If your Pals are constantly exhausted, your automation is not sustainable. Add more rest areas and food supply.

3. **Over-specializing too early.** Make sure your basic production chains are stable before investing in advanced automation.

4. **Forgetting about power and infrastructure.** Some endgame workstations require power or specific environmental conditions. Plan utilities into your layout from the start.

5. **Not using Pal traits effectively.** Some Pals have passive traits that boost specific work types. Always check individual Pal traits, not just species averages.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best base layout for automation in Palworld?

The hub-and-spoke design is the most efficient layout for automation. Place a central storage hub with dedicated production spokes branching outward. This minimizes Pal travel time between workstations and storage, which is the biggest factor in production speed.

### Which Pals are the best workers for base automation?

The best workers depend on the task. For smelting, Arsox and Jormuntide Ignis are top-tier. For crafting, Gedris and Relaxaurus excel. For mining, look for Tombat and Digtoise. Always check individual Pal work suitability ratings rather than relying on species reputation alone.

### How do I keep my working Pals from running out of SAN?

Provide adequate food supply, rest areas, and implement a Pal rotation system. Assign Pals in shifts so they alternate between working and resting. Hot springs near rest areas provide rapid SAN recovery. Keep high-SAN food like mushroom stew and meat pies stocked at feeding stations.

### How many Pals do I need for a fully automated base?

A functional automated base requires at minimum 20-30 Pals covering all work categories. A true endgame megabase typically runs 60-120 Pals with dedicated workers for every production chain, plus reserves for rotation and SAN management.

### Can I automate resource gathering as well as crafting?

You can automate base-based crafting and processing, but raw resource gathering (mining, logging) in the open world still requires active play or sending Pals on expeditions. Build mining and logging outposts near resource nodes and have hauler Pals transport materials back to your main base for processing.
