# Palworld Breeding Guide: Complete Mechanics & Perfect Pal Breeding

Palworld breeding is one of the most complex and rewarding systems in the game. Unlike simply catching wild Pals, breeding gives you control over offspring species, passive skills (IVs), and individual values (Attack, Defense, HP). This Palworld breeding guide covers everything from basic mechanics to breeding perfect 4-golden-IV Pals with max stats.

Whether you want to create the ultimate worker Pal with Craftsmanship, Serious, Workaholic, and Rare passives, or breed a perfect Griffin for combat, this guide has you covered.

## How Breeding Works in Palworld

Breeding in Palworld requires placing one male and one female Pal inside a Breeding Farm, along with at least one Cake. After a set period, the Pals produce an Egg that hatches into a new Pal.

### Basic Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Breeding Farm | Unlocked at Technology level 19 |
| Cooking Pot | Unlocked at Technology level 17 (for Cake) |
| One male + one female Pal | Must be compatible species |
| Cake | Consumed every 5 minutes during breeding |
| 2 Pal slots | At your base |

### Key Mechanics

- **Species inheritance**: Each Pal has a hidden "Breeding Power" value. The offspring species is determined by averaging the parents' Breeding Power and finding the closest match.
- **Night breeding**: If both parents are nocturnal, breeding continues at night. Otherwise, progress stops while either parent sleeps.
- **No hunger/SAN needed**: Pals inside the Breeding Farm do not consume food or lose SAN.
- **Egg cap**: The Breeding Farm holds a maximum of 20 Eggs. Remove them regularly to keep production running.

## Cake Recipe & Materials

Cake is the fuel for breeding. One Cake requires 2,000 work units, making efficient Cake production critical for serious breeders.

### Cake Ingredients

| Material | Quantity | Source |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Red Berries | 8 | Farm or purchase |
| Flour | 5 (from 15 Wheat) | Farm Wheat → Mill |
| Egg | 8 | Ranch (Penking, Cattiva) |
| Milk | 7 | Ranch (Reindox) |
| Honey | 2 | Ranch (Beeguard) or purchase |

### Cake Production Tips

- **Fire level matters**: Each fire level increase boosts efficiency by 300%. Prioritize getting a Level 3 fire Pal ASAP.
- **Best early fire Pals**: Bushi or Foxparks (Level 2 fire). Upgrade to Fuddler (Level 3) by moving eggs from the Volcano area.
- **Late-game fire Pals**: Blazehowl and Helzephyr are top-tier for Cake production.
- **Worker passives**: Prioritize 4× work speed passives (Craftsmanship, Workaholic, Serious, Rare) on your baking Pals.
- **Best food for workers**: Salad (2 Lettuce + 2 Tomato) boosts work speed significantly.

## Passive Skill (IV) Inheritance

Passive skills are what separate a good Pal from a perfect one. Here is how inheritance works:

### Inheritance Probabilities

| Number of Passives Inherited | Probability |
|------------------------------|-------------|
| 4 passives | 10% |
| 3 passives | 20% |
| 2 passives | 30% |
| 1 passive | 40% |

- All unique passives from both parents form the pool
- Each passive in the pool has an equal chance of being selected
- If fewer than 4 unique passives exist in the pool, all are inherited
- 60% chance of mutation (random new passives added) if fewer than 4 passives inherited
- **Only two white (no-passive) parents can produce a white offspring**

### Individual Values (IVs)

Each Pal has Attack, Defense, and HP IVs ranging from 0-100:
- 33% chance to inherit from father
- 33% chance to inherit from mother
- 33% chance to randomize (normal distribution 0-100)

## Setting Up a Breeding Base

A dedicated breeding base is essential for efficient production. Here is the optimal setup:

### Location Requirements
- Choose a flat area with **no enemy raids**
- No trees or rocks nearby (they interfere with Pal pathing)
- Cover the ground with foundations

### Layout
- Place 7-10 Breeding Farms around the Pal terminal
- Maximum 8 farms per floor; stack 2 floors for 10+ farms
- Do NOT place beds, hot springs, or work facilities inside the farm — Pals get stuck
- Server default: max 15 working Pals (20 with host settings)

## How to Breed a 4-Golden Pal (Perfect Passives)

A "4-golden" Pal has exactly four desired passives with no unwanted ones. Here is the step-by-step method:

### Step 1: Find Clean Parents

Search your Pal Box for Pals that only have your target passives (or a subset of them). Any extra "junk" passives will pollute the breeding pool.

### Step 2: Combine Passives Toward Target

Work backward from your target Pal using a breeding calculator. Combine passives in 2+2 or 3+1 formations for efficiency.

**Example: Breeding a 4-golden Blazamut (Craftsmanship/Workaholic/Serious/Lucky)**
1. Find a Pal with one target passive → breed toward Night Hawk (Blazamut ingredient)
2. Find another Pal with different target passives → breed toward Foxparks (Blazamut ingredient)
3. Combine the two intermediate Pals to get Blazamut with 4 goldens

### Step 3: Purify Junk Passives (If Needed)

If you cannot find clean parents:
- Breed your Pal with a white/duplicate-passive Pal
- 8% success rate for 2-select-1 purification
- Catching new wild Pals is usually more efficient

## Breeding Perfect IV Pals (4-Golden + Max Stats)

Perfect IV Pals have 4 desired passives AND 100 in all three IVs (Attack, Defense, HP). Expect to hatch 135-200+ Eggs.

### Strategy
1. Start with clean-passive Pals that have at least one max IV (100)
2. Breed two half-perfect Pals together — the more max IVs the parents share, the better
3. Use intermediate "half-perfect" Pals found during the process to run parallel breeding farms
4. Install an IV display MOD and Pal terminal expansion MOD for this stage

### Optimization
- Run multiple Breeding Farms simultaneously with intermediate Pals
- Any offspring with 4 passives + at least one max IV can contribute to another farm
- Expected Egg count drops below 135 with proper optimization

## Breeding FAQ

### Why is my Breeding Farm not producing Eggs?

Check these in order:
- Are both Pals the correct genders?
- Are both Pals within the Breeding Farm range?
- Is one of the Pals sleeping? (Progress stops)
- Is the Cake placed in the Farm's built-in chest?
- Have you reached the 20-Egg cap? Remove Eggs to continue.

### How long does breeding take?

Under default settings, one Cake is consumed every 5 minutes, producing one Egg. Nocturnal Pals breed 24/7; mixed sleep schedules halve production.

### Do breeding Pals need food and beds?

No. The developer removed hunger, SAN, and bed requirements inside the Breeding Farm in an update. However, this may change in future patches.

### Where can I look up breeding recipes?

Use online tools like [Caimogu Breeding Calculator](https://palworld.caimogu.cc/breed.html) to find exact parent combinations for any target Pal.
