# Palworld Pal Encyclopedia: Top 20 Most Useful Pals for Every Stage

Building the right Pal roster in Palworld is the single biggest factor separating smooth playthroughs from painful grinds. With over 200 Pals scattered across every biome, it is easy to waste hours chasing flashy creatures while your base grinds to a halt. This Palworld Pal encyclopedia cuts through the noise and ranks the 20 most useful Pals you should own at every stage of the game — from your first day to endgame raids.

Each entry below includes where to find the Pal, what role it fills, which passive skills make it great, and why it earns its spot on this list. Whether you need a tireless miner, a fire-breathing mount, or a breeding partner that produces godlike offspring, this guide has you covered.

## Why These 20 Pals Matter

Palworld rewards versatility. A single Pal might be excellent at combat but useless at base work, or incredible at farming but fragile in a fight. The 20 Pals on this list were chosen because they cover every major gameplay loop:

- **Combat** — Boss fights, tower raids, PvP arenas
- **Base Work** — Mining, watering, planting, harvesting, crafting
- **Riding** — Ground and aerial traversal across the map
- **Breeding** — Passing down elite passives to offspring

If you own all 20, you will never hit a wall in Palworld. Let us dive in.

## 1. Cattiva (棉悠悠) — The Early Game Workhorse

![Cattiva](/images/pals/cattiva.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Neutral |
| Role | Base worker, early game jack-of-all-trades |
| Location | Everywhere in the starting zone |
| Best Passives | Diligent, Workaholic, Serious |

Cattiva is the first Pal most players catch, and for good reason. This small Neutral-type creature spawns almost everywhere in the starting area and has surprisingly balanced work stats across multiple skill types. While it will never be your endgame powerhouse, Cattiva keeps your first base running smoothly through the early hours.

**Why it is useful:** Cattiva can handle logging, stone mining, medicine production, and handcrafting — a rare spread for a low-level Pal. Catch three or four of them on day one and your initial base will practically run itself.

**Best passives to look for:** Diligent (work speed up), Workaholic (no rest needed for longer), and Serious (focused work bonus). Any Cattiva with two of these three is worth keeping.

## 2. Penking (皮皮鸡) — The Lifetime Ranch Worker

![Penking](/images/pals/penking.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Water |
| Role | Base worker (watering, mining, transport) |
| Location | Rivers and lakes across the map |
| Best Passives | Work Slave, Artisan, Legend |

Penking earns its reputation as one of the best base Pals in the entire game. This penguin-type Water Pal has elite stats in watering, mining, and transport — three of the most in-demand work types once you hit mid-game. A Penking with the right passives can work for hours without rest and produce resources at incredible speed.

**Why it is useful:** Unlike many specialists, Penking covers multiple critical work categories. One Penking replaces the need for two or three single-purpose Pals, saving food, base slots, and management overhead.

**Best passives to look for:** Work Slave (massive work speed boost at the cost of attack), Artisan (production speed up), and Legend (work speed up). A Penking with Work Slave + Artisan is a ranch dream.

## 3. Flopie (翠叶鼠) — The Breeding Base Essential

![Flopie](/images/pals/flopie.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Grass |
| Role | Grass worker, breeding partner |
| Location | Grasslands and forest edges |
| Best Passives | Diligent, Breath of the Wind |

Flopie does not look impressive, but experienced players know this Grass-type rodent is a breeding essential. Flopie carries the rare "Breath of the Wind" passive, which boosts movement speed for all Pals at the base when even one Flopie is present. That alone makes it worth keeping a pair on your ranch.

**Why it is useful:** Beyond the speed aura, Flopie has solid grass work stats for planting and harvesting. If you run a large farming operation, a few Flopies will keep your crop fields productive around the clock.

**Best passives to look for:** Breath of the Wind (unique aura passive), Diligent, and any work speed boost. Keep at least two Flopies permanently at your breeding base.

## 4. Foxparks (火绒狐) — Early Fire Coverage

![Foxparks](/images/pals/foxparks.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Fire |
| Role | Early fire combat, kindling |
| Location | Forests near volcanic regions |
| Best Passives | Ferocious, Muscular |

Foxparks is your gateway to Fire-type gameplay. This small fox Pal learns fire attacks early and can handle kindling work at your base. While it will be outclassed by later fire Pals, Foxparks carries you through the first two zones and teaches you how Fire-type matchups work.

**Why it is useful:** Fire coverage is essential against Grass and Ice bosses, and Foxparks is the easiest Fire Pal to obtain in the first few hours. It also has decent kindling stats, so it doubles as a base worker.

**Best passives to look for:** Ferocious (attack up) and Muscular (attack speed up) for combat-oriented Foxparks, or Diligent for a work-focused build.

## 5. Dumud (企丸王) — The Mining Specialist

![Dumud](/images/pals/dumud.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Water/Earth |
| Role | Mining, base worker |
| Location | Caves and underground areas |
| Best Passives | Work Slave, Artisan, Legend |

Dumud is the Pal you want when you need ore — lots of it. This Water/Earth dual-type Pal has some of the highest mining stats in the game and can tunnel through rock nodes with incredible efficiency. If your base is drowning in a stone and ore shortage, Dumud is the solution.

**Why it is useful:** Mining bottlenecks are the single most common problem in mid-game Palworld. A ranch of three to four Dumuds with Work Slave passives will produce more ore per hour than almost any other setup.

**Best passives to look for:** Work Slave (non-negotiable), Artisan, and Legend. A perfect Dumud with all three is a mining factory.

## 6. Reindox (波霸牛) — Ranch Life and Milk

![Reindox](/images/pals/reindox.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Grass |
| Role | Ranch worker, milk production |
| Location | Plains and grassland biomes |
| Best Passives | Dairy Farmer, Robust |

Reindox is the backbone of any serious ranch operation. This Grass-type bovine produces milk regularly and has solid farming work stats. More importantly, Reindox is one of the few Pals that can be assigned to ranch facilities for passive resource generation.

**Why it is useful:** Milk is a critical crafting ingredient in mid and late-game recipes. A small herd of Reindox ensures a steady supply without constant foraging. They also handle planting and harvesting competently.

**Best passives to look for:** Dairy Farmer (increases milk yield), Robust (health and stamina up), and any work speed passives for when they are not producing milk.

## 7. Lylepanda (百合女王) — The Farming Queen

![Lylepanda](/images/pals/lylepanda.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Grass |
| Role | Elite farming, planting |
| Location | Deep forest and mountain regions |
| Best Passives | Artisan, Work Slave, Legend |

Lylepanda lives up to its name as the queen of farming in Palworld. This Grass-type giant has the highest planting stat in the game and can cultivate entire crop fields with minimal downtime. If you are running the farming builds that power endgame crafting, Lylepanda is mandatory.

**Why it is useful:** Endgame potions, armor repairs, and cooking recipes require massive quantities of crops. A Lylepanda ranch with Artisan passives can produce hundreds of crops per in-game day, eliminating one of the biggest resource bottlenecks.

**Best passives to look for:** Artisan (production speed), Work Slave (maximum work rate), and Legend (work speed multiplier).

| Role | Watering, combat support |

![Katress](/images/pals/katress.png)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Dark |
| Role | Night harvesting, dark combat |
| Location | Dark caves and nighttime spawns |
| Best Passives | Night Owl, Muscular, Diligent |

Katress has a unique niche: it harvests crops at night when other Pals are sleeping. This Dark-type creature effectively doubles your farming output if you assign it to nighttime shifts. Its combat abilities are also surprisingly potent against Light-type bosses.

**Why it is useful:** Most farming guides overlook night shifts entirely. By assigning Katress to your farm after sunset, you keep production running around the clock. This is a game-changer for players pushing endgame crafting recipes.

**Best passives to look for:** Night Owl (unique passive that boosts night work), Muscular, and Diligent.

|-----------|---------|

| Role | Ground mount, fire combat |

Faleris is the mount Pal that every player dreams of finding. This Fire-type creature has one of the fastest ground sprint speeds in the game, making it the best non-flying mount for crossing large distances quickly.

A Faleris with the Fleet Foot passive can cross the entire map in a fraction of the time it takes on foot or with ordinary mounts. Combine that with its fire combat abilities, and you have a Pal that fights as fast as it runs.

**Best passives to look for:** Fleet Foot (speed boost), Noble (stats up), and Muscular for combat.

## How to Build Your Pal Roster

Owning these 20 Pals is just the beginning. Here is how to integrate them into your gameplay:

1. **Week 1:** Catch Cattiva, Foxparks, Rooby, Teafant, and Pengullet. These give you basic coverage across all roles.
2. **Week 2:** Add Penking, Dumud, Flopie, Gobfin, and Digtoise to round out your base workers and breeding stock.
3. **Week 3:** Hunt for Reindox, Lylepanda, Relaxaurus, and Katress to maximize your farming and resource production.
4. **Week 4+:** Acquire Blazamut, Anubis, Faleris, Griffen, Verdash, and Galeclaw for endgame combat and mobility.

For more detailed information on team building, check out our [Mid-Game Team Composition Guide](/en/guides/mid-game-team-composition-guide) and [Endgame Team Composition Guide](/en/guides/endgame-team-composition-guide).

## Breeding Tips for Elite Pals

Many of the Pals on this list become exponentially better through breeding. Here are the key principles:

- **Stack Work Slave + Artisan** on any worker Pal for maximum production speed
- **Pass down Fleet Foot** to your mount Pals through careful Gobfin or Verdash breeding
- **Keep breeding stock separate** from active workers to avoid accidental deaths
- **Use the Breeding Farm** at your main base for all breeding operations

## FAQ

### What is the single most useful Pal in Palworld?

Penking is widely considered the most useful Pal overall due to its exceptional multi-category work stats (watering, mining, transport) and the Work Slave passive. A single Penking can replace several specialized workers at your base.

### Do I need all 20 Pals to enjoy Palworld?

No. You can complete the main story with far fewer Pals. However, owning all 20 eliminates every common bottleneck and makes the late game significantly smoother. Think of this list as a goal to work toward rather than a requirement.

### Which Pal should I catch first?

Cattiva and Foxparks are the best first catches. Cattiva gives you immediate base work coverage, while Foxparks provides essential Fire-type combat for early bosses. Both spawn in or near the starting area.

### Are the breeding Pals really necessary?

If you want optimized endgame Pals with perfect passives, yes. Breeding is the only way to obtain Pals with stacked Legendary passives. Gobfin, Teafant, Digtoise, and Pengullet give you accessible entry points into every major breeding line.

### What is the best mount Pal?

Faleris and Verdash are the top two ground mounts, with Faleris edging ahead for combined combat and speed. For aerial mobility, Galeclaw is the most accessible flying mount in the early game.

### Where can I find Blazamut?

Blazamut spawns in the deepest areas of the Volcano region. You need fire resistance gear and a strong combat team to reach and capture it. Plan for at least level 40+ before attempting this encounter.
