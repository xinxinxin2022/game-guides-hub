# Palworld Mid Game Best Team Composition Guide

The mid-game is where most Palworld players hit a wall. The Pals that carried you through the first 10 hours start falling behind, bosses become significantly harder, and you realize your team needs a complete overhaul. This Palworld mid game best team composition guide shows you exactly which Pals to use during this critical transition period and how to build a team that can handle everything from mid-game bosses to early late-game content.

The mid-game spans roughly from level 20 to level 40. During this phase, you need Pals that are accessible, powerful, and versatile. The Pals recommended here are specifically chosen because they bridge the gap between early-game starters and late-game legendaries, giving you a smooth progression path.

## Understanding the Mid-Game Transition

The mid-game transition is the period where you move from basic type coverage to specialized team roles. In the early game, any Pal that deals damage works fine. In the mid-game, you need to think about:

- **Type synergy** — Pals that cover each other's weaknesses
- **Role diversity** — Tank, DPS, support, and utility
- **Scaling potential** — Pals that remain useful into the late game
- **Base utility** — Pals that contribute to your base while not in your party

> **Pro Tip:** The biggest mistake mid-game players make is chasing legendary Pals too early. A well-built team of mid-tier Pals will outperform a poorly-built team of legendaries every time.

## The Core Mid-Game Team: Five Essential Pals

Our recommended mid-game team consists of five Pals that cover all major roles and type matchups. Each one is obtainable during the mid-game and scales well into later content.

### 1. Penking — The Water Tank

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Water |
| Evolution | Evolves from Teafant at level 25 |
| Role | Tank, sustained DPS |
| Best Passives | Tank, Vampiric, Stout |
| Catch Location | Evolve Teafant or catch in southern wetlands |
| Recommended Level | 28-35 |

Penking is the crown jewel of mid-game teams. This Water-type penguin Pal serves as your primary tank, absorbing massive amounts of damage while dealing consistent Water-type DPS. Its partner skill boosts the attack power of nearby Pals, making it a force multiplier for the entire team.

Penking's greatest strength is its versatility. It functions as a tank, a damage dealer, and a base worker (it has excellent Water-type base skills). Most importantly, it evolves from Teafant, which is one of the easiest Pals to catch in the early game.

**Why start with Teafant?** Teafant is available from the first hour of gameplay in coastal areas. By leveling it to level 25 during the early game, you unlock Penking just as you enter the mid-game. This gives you a head start on team building without needing to hunt for rare Pals.

### 2. Jormuntide — The Dragon Artillery

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Ice / Dragon |
| Role | Ranged DPS, area control |
| Best Passives | Muscular, Ferocious, Legend |
| Catch Location | Northern ocean, Frozen Archipelago |
| Recommended Level | 32-40 |

Jormuntide is the most powerful non-legendary Pal available in the mid-game. This Ice/Dragon-type sea serpent deals devastating area damage and has one of the highest base attack stats in the game. While technically a sacred beast, it is accessible enough that well-prepared mid-game teams can capture it.

Jormuntide serves as your team's primary ranged damage dealer. Its Ice attacks freeze enemies in place, while its Dragon attacks deal massive single-target damage. In boss fights, Jormuntide can deal more damage than any other mid-game Pal.

> **Pro Tip:** Jormuntide is expensive to maintain — it eats a lot of food. Make sure your base food production can support it before adding it to your permanent team.

### 3. Kitsun — The Speed Demon

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Ice |
| Role | Fast DPS, flanking |
| Best Passives | Swift, Nimble, Ferocious |
| Catch Location | Snowy fields, northern tundra |
| Recommended Level | 25-33 |

Kitsun is the fastest Pal in the mid-game tier. This Ice-type fox Pal covers ground quickly, flanks enemies effectively, and delivers rapid bursts of damage. Its partner skill increases movement speed for your entire team when it is in your active party.

Kitsun excels at hit-and-run tactics. dart in, deal damage, and retreat before enemies can respond. This playstyle is crucial for tougher mid-game bosses that can one-shot slower Pals.

### 4. Relaxaurus — The Support Powerhouse

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Water / Dragon |
| Role | Support, secondary DPS |
| Best Passives | Vampiric, Artisan, Serious |
| Catch Location | River deltas, central wetlands |
| Recommended Level | 27-35 |

Relaxaurus is the Swiss Army knife of mid-game teams. This Water/Dragon-type Pal has surprisingly strong combat abilities but truly shines as a support unit. Its partner skill provides healing over time to nearby Pals, keeping your team alive during extended fights.

Relaxaurus also has excellent base work stats, making it a valuable base member when not in your active party. Its dual typing gives it unique coverage that complements the rest of the team.

### 5. Bushi — The Close-Range Specialist

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Fire |
| Role | Melee DPS, boss killer |
| Best Passives | Musclehead, Vanguard, Ferocious |
| Catch Location | Volcanic foothills, eastern mountains |
| Recommended Level | 30-38 |

Every team needs a dedicated melee damage dealer, and Bushi fills that role perfectly. This Fire-type warrior Pal deals massive close-range damage and has excellent mobility for dodging boss attacks.

Bushi's partner skill increases attack power when HP is low, rewarding aggressive play. Skilled players can keep Bushi in the "low HP, high damage" zone with careful healing management, maximizing its DPS output.

### Type Coverage Matrix

| Penking (Water) | Fire, Ground, Rock | Electric, Grass | Jormuntide, Relaxaurus |
| Jormuntide (Ice/Dragon) | Grass, Flying, Dragon | Dragon, Fairy | Penking, Bushi |

As you can see, every Pal's weakness is covered by at least two teammates. This redundancy means you can always switch to a favorable type matchup regardless of the enemy.

### Recommended Switch Order

| Situation | Switch To | Reason |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Enemy is Fire-type | Penking or Relaxaurus | Water resists Fire |
| Enemy is Water-type | Jormuntide or Kitsun | Dragon/Ice beats Water |
| Boss fight (sustained) | Start with Penking | Tank can absorb early hits |
| Multiple enemies | Jormuntide | Area damage clears groups |
| Single tough boss | Bushi + Kitsun flanking | Melee burst + speed |

## How to Transition from Early Game

If you followed our beginner guide, you likely have a team built around Rushoar, Cattiva, and maybe Pengullet. Here is how to smoothly transition to the mid-game team.

### Phase 1: Catch Teafant (Hours 1-5)

If you have not already, catch a Teafant during your first few hours. Level it alongside your main combat Pal. By hour 5, Teafant should be level 15-20.

### Phase 2: Evolve to Penking (Hours 10-15)

Level Teafant to 25 and evolve it into Penking. Immediately replace your secondary tank or Water-type Pal with Penking. It will serve you well into the late game.

### Phase 3: Add Kitsun (Hours 15-20)

Travel to the northern tundra and catch a Kitsun. It provides the speed your team likely lacks at this point. Replace Pengullet or a similar early-game mount with Kitsun.

### Phase 4: Acquire Relaxaurus (Hours 20-25)

Explore the central wetlands to find Relaxaurus. This Pal fills the support gap most mid-game teams have. Replace one of your early-game utility Pals.

### Phase 5: Hunt Bushi (Hours 25-30)

By now you should be ready for the volcanic foothills. Catch Bushi to complete your five-Pal team. Replace your early-game primary DPS if it is falling behind.

### Phase 6: Capture Jormuntide (Hours 30-40)

Jormuntide is the capstone of your mid-game team. Once you have the other four Pals built up and working well, prepare for the Jormuntide hunt. This replaces any remaining early-game Pals in your roster.

> **Pro Tip:** Do not release your early-game Pals immediately. Reassign them to your base as workers. A well-leveled Rushoar makes an excellent base guard, and Cattivas are always useful as base workers.

## Breeding Your Mid-Game Team

Once you have caught your core team, improve them through breeding. Here are the top breeding priorities for mid-game Pals.

### Priority Breeding Targets

| Pal | Desired Passives | Breeding Partner |
|-----|-----------------|------------------|
| Penking | Tank + Vampiric | Other Water-types |
| Jormuntide | Muscular + Ferocious | Dragon-types |
| Kitsun | Swift + Nimble | Ice-types |
| Relaxaurus | Artisan + Serious | Water or Dragon-types |
| Bushi | Musclehead + Vanguard | Fire-types |

Breeding these Pals for passives takes multiple generations. Start with two Pals that each carry one desired passive, breed them, then breed the best offspring with another Pal carrying the remaining passives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best team composition for mid-game Palworld?

The best mid-game team consists of Penking (tank), Jormuntide (ranged DPS), Kitsun (speed DPS), Relaxaurus (support), and Bushi (melee DPS). This composition covers all major type matchups and provides balanced roles for every combat situation.

### When should I transition from my early-game team?

Start transitioning around hour 10-15 when you enter the mid-game zones. The key indicator is when your early-game Pals start getting one-shot by enemies that are only slightly above their level. This means it is time to upgrade.

### Is Jormuntide worth catching in the mid-game?

Yes, Jormuntide is absolutely worth catching in the mid-game. It is the most powerful non-legendary Pal available during this phase and remains competitive even in late-game content. Its area damage and Ice/Dragon typing provide unmatched versatility.

### Can I use the same team for base work and combat?

Partially. Penking and Relaxaurus have excellent base work stats and can serve dual roles. However, your dedicated combat Pals like Bushi and Jormuntide are better kept in your party. Maintain separate base workers for crafting, mining, and farming.

### How do I make my mid-game Pals stronger through breeding?

Focus on breeding for passive skills. Each generation of breeding can pass down better passives. Prioritize combat passives (Musclehead, Ferocious) for DPS Pals and work passives (Serious, Artisan) for base workers. It typically takes 3-5 generations to produce a Pal with four optimal passives.

### Should I save resources for late-game Pals instead?

No. Mid-game Pals are essential for reaching the late game in the first place. You need a strong team to access late-game areas and capture legendary Pals. Invest in your mid-game team fully — the resources spent are not wasted because many mid-game Pals remain useful as base workers or support units even in the late game.
